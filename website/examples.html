Real-World Examples

See how Keep It Krispy helps you work with meeting transcripts in Claude.

After a Business Call

Just finished a call and need to follow up? Ask Claude.

You What was my last meeting about?
Claude Your last meeting was a Teams call at 9:59 AM today with Guy Farber, Ken Smythe, and Sandeep Chellingi. It lasted about 20 minutes.

Based on the transcript, you discussed:
  • Project timeline adjustments for Q1
  • Budget reallocation for the infrastructure upgrade
  • Action item: Ken to send updated cost estimates by Friday
You What did Ken commit to?
Claude Ken committed to:
  1. Send updated cost estimates by Friday (Jan 17)
  2. Review the vendor proposals and provide a recommendation
  3. Schedule a follow-up with the infrastructure team next week

Weekly Meeting Summary

  • 9:59 AM - Team sync (20 min) - Q1 timeline, budget discussion

Key themes: Budget planning, Q1 priorities, infrastructure upgrades.

Semantic Search

Find meetings by meaning, not just keywords.

You When did we talk about budget concerns?
Claude I found 2 meetings where budget concerns were discussed:

Jan 14 - Team sync (9:59 AM)
"...we need to be careful about the spending on infrastructure. The current estimates are 15% over what we budgeted for Q1..."

Jan 10 - Planning session (2:30 PM)
"...cost overruns from last quarter are a concern. We should review vendor contracts before committing to the upgrade..."
How semantic search works: The query "budget concerns" also finds mentions of "cost overruns", "spending too much", "over budget", etc. It understands meaning, not just exact words.

Claude Here's a draft follow-up email:

Let me know if I missed anything.

Best,
[Your name]

  • You were going to approve training budget (pending)
  • She was going to finish auth documentation

Correct Unknown Speakers

Krisp sometimes labels participants as "Speaker 1", "Speaker 2", etc. You can tell Claude to fix this.

You In my last meeting, Speaker 2 was actually John Smith from Acme Corp. Can you fix that?
Claude I've updated the speaker mapping for that meeting. "Speaker 2" is now identified as John Smith.

This correction will be applied automatically whenever you view this transcript. The name will also be replaced throughout the transcript text.

Would you like me to add a LinkedIn URL for John Smith for future reference?
You Yes, his LinkedIn is linkedin.com/in/johnsmith. Also, Speaker 1 was Jane Doe.
Claude Done! I've updated both speakers:
  • Speaker 1 → Jane Doe
  • Speaker 2 → John Smith (LinkedIn: linkedin.com/in/johnsmith)

These corrections are saved and will apply to this transcript going forward.
How it works: Speaker corrections are stored in DynamoDB and automatically applied when you fetch transcripts. The corrected names replace the original names both in the speaker list and throughout the transcript text.
